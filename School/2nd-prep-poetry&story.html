Poetry: Blow, Blow, Thou Winter Wind

Stanza 2

Freeze, freeze, thou bitter sky,
That dost not bite so nigh
As benefits forgot:
Though thou the waters warp,
Thy sting is not so sharp
As a friend remembered not.

1. Vocabulary:

Bitter: Unpleasant / HarshNigh: Near
Dost not: Does notBenefits: Favours / Gratitude

2. Paraphrase:

The poet speaks to the cold harsh sky. He believes that the bite of the winter cold is not as painful as the unkindness of ungrateful people. What hurts more than the cold is when people forget your kindness. [span_0](start_span)Even the freezing wind that warps water isn't as sharp as a friend who forgets the relationship.[span_0](end_span)
